Chicken Soup

(↑This is just a random pic cuz I can't import images from other websites.)


  • Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Serves: 10 to 20 cups
Ingredients Quantity
olive oil 1 tablespoon
onion 1
celery stalks 2
carrots 2
low-soduim chicken broth 8 cups
brown rice 1/2 cup
cooked chicken 2 cups

Step 1:
Put the pot on the stove and turn the heat to medium.
When it is hot (flick some water on-it should dance and evaporate immediately) carefully add the oil.

Step 2:
Add the onion, celery, and carrots, and cook until tender, 10-15 minutes.

Step 3:
Add the chicken broth, raise the heat to high and bring to a boil. Lower the heat to low, and cook, uncovered, until the vegetables are tender and no longer float on the top, about 30 minutes.

Step 4:
Add the rice, barley or pasta and cook until tender, about 20 minutes for pasta, 40 minutes for brown rice or barley.
(If you want you can use leftover grains or pasta . If so, skip this step and add them when you add the chicken, step 5. This will also cut 20 minutes off the cooking time.)

Step 5:
Add the chicken, stir, and cook until heated throughout, about 3 minutes.

Step 6:
Serve right away or cover and refrigerate up to 3 days.

Source: ChopChop Magazine - Easy Chicken Soup

Deviled Eggs

(↑This is just a random pic cuz I can't import images from other websites.)


  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
plain low-fat Greek yogurt 2 tablespoons
mustard 1 teaspoon
fresh herbs (e.g. parsley, basil, dill, tarragon, or cilantro) 1 tablespoon
salt 1/8 teaspoon
large eggs (hard-cooked and boiled) 4

Step 1:
Put the yogurt, mustard, herbs, and salt in the small bowl and set aside.

Step 2:
Cut the eggs in half lengthwise. Remove the yolks (they’ll pop out if you push the white underneath them) and put them in the bowl with the yogurt and mash until coarse or creamy, whichever you prefer.

Step 3:
Put the whites on a plate. Use a spoon to refill each egg half with the yolk mixture and sprinkle with extra herbs, if you like.

Source: ChopChop Magazine - Deviled Eggs

Fish Tacos

(↑This is just a random pic cuz I can't import images from other websites.)


  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
ground cumin 1 teaspoon
chili powder 1 teaspoon
kosher salt 1/4 teaspoon
garlic (peeled and minced) 1 clove
1⁄2 poundhalibut fillets or other firm white fish 1
(6-inch) corn tortillas 8
Purple Cabbage Slaw
onion 1/2
tomato 1 cup
avocado 1 cup
fresh cilantro leaves 1/2 cup
lime 1
hot sauce
plain Greek yogurt

Step 1:
Put the oil, spices, salt, and garlic in the bowl and mix well.

Step 2:
Cut the fish into 1-inch strips, put them in the bowl, and use your clean fingers to coat them with the spice mixture.
Set the fish aside.
Be sure to wash your hands with soap and water after handling raw fish!

Step 3:
Put the skillet on the stove and turn the heat to medium.
When it is hot, add the tortillas, one at a time, and cooked until warm, about 30 seconds on each side.
Wrap them in a dish towel to keep warm.

Step 4:
Put the skillet on the stove and turn the heat to medium-high.
Add the fish and cook 3 minutes, then use the spatula to flip the pieces over.
Cook until the fish breaks easily into flakes when you poke it with a fork, about 2 minutes.

Step 5:
Give each person two tortillas and let them assemble the tacos with the toppings they like.

Source: ChopChop Magazine - Fish Tacos
